# Cash Flow

Cash flow refers to the movement of money into and out of a business over a period of time. It is an important financial indicator that shows whether a company has enough cash to operate, pay its obligations, and invest in growth.

Unlike profit, which is based on accounting calculations, cash flow focuses on the actual movement of cash.

Cash flow is typically categorized into three main activities:

### Operating Activities
Operating cash flow includes cash generated from the core business operations. This includes money received from customers and payments made for operating expenses such as wages, rent, and utilities.

### Investing Activities
Investing cash flow relates to buying or selling long-term assets. Examples include:
- Purchasing equipment
- Selling property
- Investing in securities

These activities reflect how a company invests in its future growth.

### Financing Activities
Financing cash flow involves transactions related to funding the business. Examples include:
- Receiving loans
- Issuing shares
- Paying dividends
- Repaying debt

Positive cash flow means that a company has more cash coming in than going out. Negative cash flow means the company is spending more money than it receives.

Managing cash flow effectively is critical for maintaining business operations. Even profitable companies can fail if they do not have enough cash to pay their short-term obligations.

Businesses use cash flow statements to:
- Monitor liquidity
- Plan future expenses
- Evaluate financial sustainability
- Ensure the company can meet its financial commitments

Strong cash flow management helps businesses remain stable and continue growing.
