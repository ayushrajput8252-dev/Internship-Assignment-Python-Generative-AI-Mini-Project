# Profit and Loss Statement

A Profit and Loss (P&L) statement, also known as an income statement, is a financial report that summarizes a company's revenues, costs, and expenses during a specific period. It helps businesses evaluate their financial performance and determine whether they are making a profit or experiencing a loss.

The primary purpose of the profit and loss statement is to show how effectively a business generates revenue and manages its expenses. It provides valuable insights into operational efficiency and financial sustainability.

The main components of a P&L statement include:

### Revenue
Revenue refers to the total income generated from selling goods or services. It is often called sales or turnover.

### Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
This represents the direct costs involved in producing goods or delivering services, such as raw materials and labor.

### Gross Profit
Gross profit is calculated as:

Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold

It indicates how efficiently a company produces and sells its products.

### Operating Expenses
Operating expenses include costs required to run the business, such as:
- Salaries and wages
- Rent and utilities
- Marketing expenses
- Administrative costs

### Net Profit or Net Loss
Net profit is the final result after subtracting all expenses from total revenue.

Net Profit = Total Revenue − Total Expenses

A positive result indicates profit, while a negative result indicates a loss.

Businesses use the profit and loss statement to:
- Evaluate financial performance
- Identify areas where costs can be reduced
- Plan future investments
- Provide information to investors and stakeholders

The P&L statement is typically prepared monthly, quarterly, or annually and is a key financial document for business analysis.
